About

Background and Education

I am a graduate student at UC San Diego, specialising in Intelligent Systems, Robotics, and Controls. At the Existential Robotics Lab (ERL) I work on ADMM based receding horizon control and hierarchical MPPI for contact rich, nonprehensile manipulation. At the ARC Lab I work on sim to real transfer for dexterous manipulation of articulated tools using two robot arms and NVIDIA Isaac Sim.

I completed my B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering at IIT Madras, where I worked across multiple labs on autonomous underwater vehicles, quadrotors operating across multiple environments, a walking assist exoskeleton, and RF source seeking drone navigation. The work at DiRo Lab led to a publication at ICCAS 2025.

In the summer of 2025 I worked as a Controls Engineer at Ather Energy, building state estimation and multi-rate control algorithms for battery management systems.

My core interests are in robot learning and optimal control for dexterous manipulation, spanning reinforcement learning, contact rich and nonprehensile manipulation, with a focus on sim to real transfer onto real robotic hardware.
